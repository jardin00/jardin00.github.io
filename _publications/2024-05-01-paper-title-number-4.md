I participated in the 2024 Spring Joint Conference of KORMS and KIIE.



Urban logistics systems are undergoing rapid transformation due to the development of technologies such as Urban Air Mobility (UAM), autonomous vehicles, and underground logistics. In response, various studies have been conducted on the design of urban logistics infrastructure; however, most of the existing research has focused primarily on infrastructure design linked to a single mode of transportation. This fails to adequately reflect the fact that real logistics systems require the complex integration of multiple modes of transport.

In the future, logistics will center on the collaboration of UAM, underground logistics, and ground logistics, with the goal of providing customers with rapid logistics services. Therefore, this study proposes a methodology for building a multidimensional logistics network that goes beyond reliance on a single mode of transport. Specifically, the study addresses a simultaneous facility location optimization problem that integratively considers UAM vertiports and ground logistics hubs. A multi-period, bi-objective mathematical optimization model is developed and validated using data from 426 administrative districts in Seoul.

This study advances beyond existing research on urban logistics infrastructure design by presenting a novel methodology that enables diverse modes of transportation to cooperate and integrate within future urban environments. The results are expected to enhance the efficiency of air-ground collaborative urban logistics systems and contribute to the establishment of next-generation logistics networks.
