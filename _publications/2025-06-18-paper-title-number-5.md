I participated in the 2025 Spring Joint Conference of KORMS and KIIE. And I presented my research.



Urban Air Mobility (UAM) is gaining attention as a next-generation mode of urban transportation and is expected to be used as a public transport service similar to taxis. In UAM services, it is essential to establish efficient flight operation plans based on customer service requests, and the efficient operation of vertiports for UAM takeoffs, landings, and passenger boarding and disembarkation is also crucial.

Although various studies on UAM service scheduling exist, most of the existing research has focused only on the overall capacity of vertiports (i.e., the number of UAMs that can take off and land simultaneously). Research that explicitly considers pad assignment within vertiports remains very limited, which overlooks practical constraints at the pad level and the management of charging resources.

This study defines a UAM routing problem that incorporates the number of landing/takeoff pads at each vertiport, pad occupancy times, and charging durations. A mathematical optimization model is developed to derive optimal UAM routing and pad assignments. Furthermore, to support real-time operational environments such as new service requests and cancellations, a rolling horizon approach is introduced.

The proposed model is tested using data from the K-UAM demonstration routes, confirming its applicability to real-world flight paths and highlighting the necessity of planning that accounts for pad-level constraints.
